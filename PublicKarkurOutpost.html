Karkur Outpost

The Karkur Mercenary Outpost at the Western Desert’s Edge

Carved from sun-baked sandstone and standing like a squat fortress against the endless dunes, the Karkur outpost marks the last reliable haven before caravans commit to the deep desert. Its red-and-indigo banners snap in the hot wind, echoing the colors of the famed Karkur lamellar armor. Travelers see it long before they reach it: a low wall of blocky stone, a cluster of shade-trees nourished by the precious well, and the distant glint of spearheads as mercenaries patrol the parapets.

The outpost is a crossroads of rumor and opportunity. At dawn and dusk, the desert paints the sandstone walls in vibrant gold and violet, and the outpost feels like a sturdy island rising from a sea of shifting sands. Many pass through, some stay longer than they intend.

Surface Level

The main structure serves as a bustling waystation for caravan crews, merchants, and adventurers. A wide archway leads into an open-air hall where desert winds flow freely, keeping the interior cool by day. The inn, run by Fennik Dusktankard, occupies the heart of the space with long communal tables, a sturdy bar, and a cooking pit where he prepares sizzling spiced meats and his signature cactus-ale. Fennik chats with everyone—grinning, practical, and always listening—gathering gossip like coin.

Beside the inn are spacious stables built from reinforced sandstone, tall enough for desert camels, pack lizards, and the occasional mercenary war-rig. Blackened braziers light the yard at night, casting sharp shadows over the well, whose water feeds the shade-trees that rustle like whispers in the otherwise silent dunes. A small central tower houses Keshira Stonebloom’s office, overlooking the courtyard with a commanding view.

Subterranean Levels

Much of the outpost’s true strength lies below the sands. A guarded stairwell beyond the inn leads into the cool, torch-lit depths carved directly into the bedrock.

Barracks

Orderly stone chambers house the elite Karkur soldarii. Rows of broad beds, weapon racks, and personal storage niches line the rooms. The air smells faintly of metal oil and desert herbs burned to keep the space dry and comfortable. Off-duty mercenaries dice, drill, and share stories in adjoining common rooms.

Food Stores

A carefully maintained series of storage vaults keeps dried meats, hardbread, dates, cactus-wine, and preserved desert beans safe from heat and vermin. Fennik oversees much of the supply management—he has little patience for waste—and signs of his culinary influence are everywhere: labeled spices, jars of pickled roots, and bundles of aromatic thorn-harvest.

Teleportation Chamber

The deepest chamber is sealed behind runic stone doors. Here lies a circular hall etched with shimmering geometric patterns. The guild keeps its existence quiet, revealing it only to high-value clients or mercenaries involved in distant deployments. The chamber smells faintly of ozone and old magic, and the floor hums softly when activated. Iron bars and a locked gate surround the circle itself.

People

Commander: Keshira Stonebloom

Personality: Charismatic, sharp-tongued, and unflinchingly pragmatic; has a knack for reading people instantly.

Backstory: Once a renowned desert guide, Keshira saved a Karkur mercenary captain during a raider siege and was invited into the guild. She quickly rose through the ranks.

Inn Keeper: Fennik Dusktankard

Personality: Warm-hearted, endlessly talkative, and a master of subtle gossip-gathering.

Backstory: Once the camp-cook for a Karkur strike company, Fennik retired after taking an arrow in the hip and chose to run the outpost’s inn instead of returning home. He quietly passes useful information to the commander, ensuring trouble is handled before it reaches his common room.

Quartermaster: Korim Flintcoil

Personality: Calm and quietly judgmental, Korim remembers every favor owed and every item issued, even when others forget.

Sergeant: Tovik Redwatch

Personality: Sardonic and battle-worn, Malren defuses tension with dry humor while ensuring no one underestimates the consequences of disobedience.
