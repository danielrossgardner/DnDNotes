Khaz-Voruun — The City of the Burning Breath

Summary: Carved into blood-red canyon walls, Khaz-Voruun is a dwarven bastion of forges, cisterns, and carved facades. Silent most days except for the monthly caravan rites, the city channels dragon imagery, ornate metalwork, and a ritual balance of fire and water in its architecture and culture.

Approach & Serpent Hall

The canyon that leads to Khaz-Voruun is typically eerily silent, broken only by scheduled caravans. Travelers enter the city through the Serpent Hall — a long, vaulted corridor where twin serpentine dragons coil in relief along opposite walls. Each dragon’s eye is set with topaz, each scale etched with scenes from dwarven legend. The hall opens into a great chamber with a massive steel door, its surface engraved with the Emberthane history.

City Layout

Beyond the great door the city branches into three main paths:

  • Right Path — Market Vaults: Lantern-lit corridors and vaulted stalls where lacquered metalwork, indigo desert-woven cloth, spices, and fine desert goods are traded. The smell of oil, pepper, and brass dust hangs heavy.
  • Left Path — Mines of Ember Vein: A descending artery into the workshop levels and ore veins, with vent tunnels carved to carry smoke away to the desert surface — the odd sight of smoke seeping from the sand hints at forges below.
  • Central Path — Palace Approach: Ascends through tiered courtyards, still-water basins, and red sand gardens toward terraces and the imperial palace.

The Palace of the Stoneflame

The palace blends strength and ceremony: steel beams curve like dragon wings above reflective pools and sand-gardens. Bronze braziers shaped as coiled serpents burn incense; terraces host quiet alcoves for counsel and ritual. The palace balances the austerity of the forge with the tempering presence of the city’s cisterns.

Rulers

Emperor Dharun Emberthane — The Stoneflame
Embodiment of the forge’s endurance; his rule emphasizes steady strength and ritual mastery of fire and metal.

Empress Kaela Emberthane — The Voice of the Cisterns
Keeper of the sacred waters and mediator between flame and life; she oversees the city’s cisterns, rites of renewal, and the balance that sustains the desert hold.

Culture & Spirit

Khaz-Voruun’s dwarves live by the principle of silent flame — power restrained until called. Reds and indigos dominate decoration, dragon motifs appear in armor and lacquer, and guards wear ornamental-but-effective lamellar with spears and broad shields. Households keep secret desert entrances, a tradition from ancient sieges and a practical safeguard.

The city’s artisans produce desert-woven cloth, fine metalwork, and spice blends traded north to the Lowroad and south toward Freeport. Smoke vents and hidden shafts shape the skyline; at dusk the carved dragons seem to watch the sands for any who would test the city’s silence.

Dwarven Cultural Colors & Symbolism

The dwarves of Khaz-Voruun express their devotion to the Twin Dragons through the colors they wear and the artistry of their craft. Each hue carries sacred meaning tied to their homeland and its spirits:

  • Red: The lifeblood of the desert and the color of the stone canyons that cradle their cities. It symbolizes endurance and the flame of the forge.
  • Indigo: Drawn from mineral-rich dye and deep wells, this color represents the hidden waters of the cisterns — calm strength and unseen vitality beneath the earth.
  • Gold: Reserved for moments of revelation and mastery, honoring Solmara the Golden Wind. Used in inlay, adornments, and ceremonial armor.
  • Azure Blue: A rare and sacred pigment kept for rites of peace, reflection, and remembrance, honoring Thalun the Silent Wave. Found in murals near the cisterns and temple pools.

During great festivals, the desert skies are lined with banners of red and indigo, but at the ceremony’s height, the priests unveil gold and azure cloths — symbolic of the dragons’ dance, uniting flame and stillness.

The Twin Dragons of the Desert

Solmara the Golden Wind

Honorific: The Breath of Knowing
Domain: Knowledge (Cunning, Action)
Tone: Brilliant, restless, and wise — Solmara embodies insight that drives motion and change.

Solmara is said to glide through the desert skies as a shimmering current of gold light, her wings scattering mirages that reveal fragments of hidden truths. When she exhales, sand lifts into intricate spirals — a sign that some secret or invention is about to emerge. Scholars and smiths alike whisper her name before attempting acts of creation. Legends tell that she once traced the first dwarven runes into the canyon walls using the light of her scales. To follow her wisdom is to move forward; to stagnate is to lose her favor.

Thalun the Silent Wave

Honorific: The Still Breath Beneath
Domain: Peace (Wisdom, Patience, Temperance)
Tone: Serene and eternal — Thalun embodies stillness, endurance, and the balance of water hidden beneath stone.

Thalun is said to dwell beneath the sands, coiling through deep aquifers and forgotten cisterns. His eyes are the cool reflections of moonlight on still water, and his passing brings soft winds that soothe the heart. During times of drought or unrest, dwarves claim to see his blue shape shimmer beneath the surface of the dunes, heralding calm rains. He is invoked in rituals of reconciliation and in moments when patience must temper flame. When Solmara and Thalun dance in the heavens, gold and blue light weave together, painting the sky violet — a sign of perfect harmony between knowledge and peace.

The Brass Warrens

A large market that sprawls through a vast network of lantern-lit corridors, their vaulted ceilings contain mosaics. Unlike the open markets of surface cities, the Brass Warrens are enclosed and labyrinthine — a city within a city — where every turn reveals another corridor of stalls pressed shoulder to shoulder, their wares spilling into the walkways in organized abundance. The smell is particular and permanent: brass dust, pepper, rendered fat from cooking fires, indigo dye, and the faint mineral tang of deep stone.

The corridors are numbered and named but the system is known only to those who have worked them for years. Strangers navigate by landmark — the stall of the one-eyed gem cutter, the corner where the spice merchants cluster, the wide junction where the lanterns burn gold instead of red. Lacquered brass catches the light from every angle. Voices echo and layer and the whole place hums with the particular energy of ten thousand small transactions happening simultaneously. It is loud, warm, and overwhelming, and dwarves navigate it with the unhurried confidence of people who have been shopping in the same corridors their entire lives.

Inns, Bars & Taverns of Khaz-Voruun

1. The Cistern Table

Location: Located at the base of the Central Path where it meets the Market Vaults.

Proprietor: Hunda Orvek (Female Dwarf, Commoner) — broad-shouldered, unhurried, and possessed of a stare that ends arguments before they start.

Atmosphere: The oldest drinking establishment in Khaz-Voruun, carved from a chamber that once served as a secondary cistern. The walls still sweat faintly with moisture and the low curved ceiling gives every conversation an intimate quality. Favored by off-duty palace guards and senior craftspeople.

Specialty: Cistern Cold — unaged spirit drawn from the deep wells and served in a stone cup with a sliver of mineral salt on the rim. Bracingly cold and aggressively simple.

2. The Serpent's Coil

Location: Situated in the upper Market Vaults near the brass and metalwork quarter.

Proprietor: Torven Ashbraid (Male Dwarf, Fighter 3, retired) — compact, scarred, and relentlessly cheerful in the way of someone who has survived things they don't discuss.

Atmosphere: Narrow and vertical, built into a natural fissure in the canyon wall with platforms connected by iron stairs. Loud, busy, and slightly dangerous to navigate after a few drinks. The walls are covered in hammered copper panels engraved with scenes of the Twin Dragons.

Specialty: Ember Bite — a dark spiced ale brewed with volcanic mineral water and a pinch of something that makes the back of the throat warm for an hour afterward.

3. The Still Pool

Location: Tucked into a quiet side corridor off the Central Path near the lower terraces.

Proprietor: Meriva Dawnstone (Female Dwarf, Cleric 4, retired, Life Domain) — gentle, precise, and quietly watchful. She forgets nothing.

Atmosphere: Hushed and deliberately peaceful, styled after the cistern shrines with small reflecting pools set into the floor and azure tile work along the lower walls. A place for negotiation, private conversation, and meetings that require discretion. No raised voices, by custom and by Meriva's enforcement.

Specialty: Thalun's Rest — a warm honeyed drink with mineral water and dried mountain herbs. Non-alcoholic. The only establishment in Khaz-Voruun where this is considered unremarkable.

4. The Forge Mouth

Location: Deep in the Left Path near the upper workshop levels.

Proprietor: Brazden Ironkeep (Male Dwarf, Fighter 5) — enormous, partially deaf from decades near active forges, communicates largely through gestures and a surprisingly expressive eyebrow.

Atmosphere: Built directly adjacent to a vent tunnel, the bar is perpetually warm and smells of coal and hot metal. The clientele are almost exclusively miners and forge workers ending long shifts. Outsiders are tolerated but stared at until they buy a round for the table.

Specialty: Black Slag — a thick dark stout so heavy it is eaten as much as drunk. Served with a heel of dense bread and a wedge of hard cheese.

5. The Gold Inlay

Location: Found in the upper Market Vaults near the gem and jewelry quarter.

Proprietor: Sulvara Gemthane (Female Dwarf, Rogue 4) — impeccably dressed, gold inlay on her rings matching her establishment's signage, and professionally impossible to read.

Atmosphere: The most expensive establishment in the Ember Vaults. Velvet-curtained booths, gold lamp fittings, and a selection of imported wines alongside the local spirits. Merchant houses conduct serious business here. The staff are exceptionally well-trained and hear everything.

Specialty: Solmara's Pour — a golden aged spirit from a private distillery, served warm in a gilt cup. Ordered by name to signal you can afford to be here.

6. The Cracked Pillar

Location: Located at the far end of the Market Vaults near the oldest corridor junction.

Proprietor: Old Durwick (Male Dwarf, Commoner, age indeterminate) — has been here longer than anyone can verify. May own the building or may simply have never left. Unclear.

Atmosphere: The oldest, cheapest, and most genuinely beloved drinking hole in the Vaults. The cracked support pillar it is named for has been braced with iron bands for two centuries and is considered good luck to touch on the way in. Regulars have carved their names into the bar top going back four generations.

Specialty: House Ale — no name, no description, no alternative. It is what it is and it costs almost nothing.

7. The Vent Room

Location: Carved into a chamber alongside an active vent tunnel in the Left Path.

Proprietors: The Askveld Brothers, Pren and Doruk (Male Dwarves, Commoners) — identical twins who finish each other's sentences and have a complete inability to agree on anything except that the other one is wrong.

Atmosphere: Perpetually smoky from the adjacent vent, with low benches and long communal tables. Popular with younger miners and apprentice smiths who come for the noise, the gambling, and the brothers' ongoing argument, which has apparently been running for eleven years.

Specialty: Smoke and Salt — a pale ale served with a smoked meat skewer. The recipe is disputed. Both brothers claim sole credit.

8. The Red Sand

Location: Situated on the lower Central Path between the market and the palace terraces.

Proprietor: Velara Stonemantle (Female Dwarf, Bard 5, College of Lore) — elegant, well-connected, and the best-informed person in Khaz-Voruun who is not employed by the palace.

Atmosphere: A performance venue as much as a bar, with a low stage cut into the far wall where musicians, poets, and storytellers perform nightly. Red sandstone walls and indigo cloth hangings give it a warmth that most canyon establishments lack. Frequented by palace staff, minor nobles, and anyone who wants to be seen.

Specialty: Canyon Wind — a dry red wine imported at considerable expense from the surface, served in a wide-mouthed copper cup.

9. The Blind Vein

Location: Hidden down an unmarked side passage off the lower Market Vaults.

Proprietor: Unknown. Staff change frequently and no one who works there discusses ownership.

Atmosphere: No signage, no lantern above the door, and a clientele that does not make eye contact with strangers. Transactions happen quietly and the bar top has no visible price list. Somehow always open. The Shore Watch equivalent in Khaz-Voruun is aware of it and has chosen, for reasons of their own, not to act.

Specialty: Whatever you need, if you know how to ask.

10. The Dragon's Breath

Location: Located at the main junction where all three paths diverge just inside the great door.

Proprietor: Karveth Duskmantle (Male Dwarf, Fighter 3) — former palace ceremonial guard, left under circumstances he won't discuss, runs his establishment with military precision and genuine hospitality.

Atmosphere: The first establishment most visitors encounter after entering the city and deliberately so — Karveth understood the value of location before he understood anything else about running a bar. High ceilings, twin dragon murals on opposing walls in red and indigo, and enough space to seat a large caravan company without crowding. Loud, welcoming, and a reliable place to get one's bearings.

Specialty: Twin Flame — two spirits served side by side in a divided cup, one warm and spiced representing Solmara, one cool and mineral representing Thalun. Drunk simultaneously by tipping the cup.
