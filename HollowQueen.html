The Hollow Queen and the Growing Gate

The Queen Beneath

Long before the Age of Echoes, there was a mortal queen — Nerathis of the Hollow Crown — whose hunger for dominion eclipsed all mercy. Legends say she ruled from the mountain fortress of Vey Lethan, where she sought to forge unity through fear. When her seers warned her that the spirits would never kneel, she ordered a massacre upon a mountain town that refused her tithe, slaughtering spirit-tenders, druids, and innocents alike.

That act of cruelty scarred the land itself. The mountain screamed. A seed of corruption took root in the blood-soaked earth, binding her soul to it even as her body perished in the uprising that followed. Her spirit, denied peace, sank into the dark between worlds. There it fed upon pain, ambition, and the echoes of mortal prayer, until it became something other.

Now, deep beneath Veythar, the Queen Beneath dreams and whispers — her will a bridge between this world and a reflection of it beyond, a chaotic realm twisting slowly into a cruel parody of creation.

The Domain Beyond

The realm linked to her essence was once a place of formless chaos — the source of corruption itself. Yet the Queen’s tether has shaped it over the ages, sculpting it into a dark mirror of Veythar’s landscapes: mountains of bone, seas of ink, and forests of whispering glass.

This realm of chaos and corruption, often called the domain beyond or Llygredd, grows more defined each time corruption spills into the mortal realm. Where spirits exist in harmony here, their reflections there are distorted parodies of themselves — driven to devour rather than balance.

The Queen’s Influence

Though bound, the Queen’s will seeps through the cracks between worlds. She cannot manifest directly, but she inhabits mortal minds — artists, scholars, dreamers, and cultists — through dreams and inspiration. Those touched by her influence often become architects of ruin, driven to construct shrines, monuments, or geometric carvings that resonate with the corruption’s frequency.

Each of these sites, when completed, acts as a key-stone in an invisible pattern across Veythar — a vast sigil that will, when complete, open the Growing Gate, merging both realms completely.

Symbols and Signs

Everywhere her touch lingers, one symbol returns again and again — its shape adapted but its pattern consistent:

A spiral descending into a hollow circle, encircled by seven lines that never meet.

Some carve it as a spiral eye, others as a well, or a crown made of broken rings — but always, it echoes descent, unity, and emptiness.

Her followers whisper her names in secret — The Hollow Crown, The Queen Beneath, The Silent Mother, The Voice Below. Common phrases or passcodes might change between groups, but always revolve around the same idea:

  • “The crown turns inward.”
  • “What is buried will reign.”
  • “All echoes end in silence.”

Ritual Sites and Structures

Throughout Veythar, ritual sites are being built, repaired, or unearthed — each drawing from a distinct local culture yet sharing uncanny geometry:

  • Shrines with inverted altars or upside-down sigils.
  • Caves where light never reaches the floor, always spiraling downward.
  • Stone circles with seven broken pillars.
  • Temples where water runs up walls instead of down.

Spirit-tenders call these places the Hollow Marks — points where the veil between worlds thins. Not all are complete, and not all are active… but each pulse makes the Queen’s Gate grow stronger.

Adventurer Clues

Characters investigating corruption might notice:

  • The same spiral symbol etched into unrelated ruins, charms, or cult markings.
  • Recurrent dreams of a woman’s voice speaking from a pool of black ink; smooth except lips that protrude.
  • Shrines whose offerings rot instantly — a sign that the Queen’s influence seeps through.
  • Fractured cult doctrines that all end with the same whispered benediction:
    “May her crown close around the world.”

The Web of Echoes — Core Function

  • The Hollow Queen’s influence forms a continent-spanning sigil made up of all Hollow Marks (ritual sites), creating a metaphysical network called the Web of Echoes.
  • Each Hollow Mark functions as a node.
  • Corrupted spirit-energy forms ley-like Threads between nodes.
  • Threads activate partially as each site becomes complete.
  • Full activation requires a ritual performed during the designated cosmic event, synchronizing all Threads in a single pulse.

Cosmic Event: The Shadowfall of the Umbral Wolf

  • Constellation: The Umbral Wolf — jagged stars depicting a great predator stalking the boundary between worlds.
  • Event: A triple eclipse (sun → moon → sun) caused by an invisible “dark mirror moon.” When it crosses before the Umbral Wolf, the Wolf’s shadow is cast across parts of Veythar.
  • Season: Autumn, when boundaries thin and twilight lingers.

Ritual Thresholds

Active Sites Outcome at the Shadowfall
0–10 The ritual fizzles; energies fail to align. The Hollow Queen’s influence weakens briefly.
11–25 An unstable rift opens. Corruption seeps through but can be sealed with swift action or counter-rituals.
25+ The rift fully tears open, allowing corrupted spirits, echoes, and blighted creatures to flood Veythar.

Tracking the Cults

A. Common Traces Found at Sites

  • Whorls of chalky dust from Llygredd.
  • Repeating spiral motifs.
  • Cold spots that chill the skin and evoke dread.

B. Fractured Cult Factions

  • Each faction interprets the Queen’s fractured voice differently.
  • Signatures: Their own spiral symbol variant, unique passphrases, and preferred site types (caves, inverted shrines, stone circles, water-temples).
  • Adventurer Interference Includes: providing false intel, inciting faction conflicts, removing key members, or corrupting required ritual materials.

C. NPC Dreamers & the Touched

  • Dream-marked mortals may speak prophecies in their sleep, unconsciously draw the sigil, or be drawn toward nearby sites.
  • Some hear humming or whispers when near active Threads.
  • Questioning or protecting such individuals reveals site directions, active cult factions, and ritual timing.
  • Some are cultists, some are innocents — all are useful clues.

5e Spells for Tracking the Web of Echoes

1. Detect Magic

  • Threads appear as faint spiraling lines of Necrotic + Transmutation energy.
  • Each site radiates a unique pattern; stronger nodes pulse rhythmically.

2. Augury

  • At Hollow Marks, omens favor the direction of the next node.
  • Casting at multiple angles can triangulate the next site.

3. Locate Object

  • Requires a ritual tool (spiral dagger, seven-rod fetish, Llygreddian stone).
  • Points directly toward the last location where such an item was used — forming a supernatural “cult trail.”

4. Detect Evil and Good

  • Threads radiate aberration or fey signatures.
  • Reveals the direction of spiritual drift along the Web.

5. Commune

  • Useful questions include:
  • “Is the nearest Hollow Mark to the north?”
  • “Which faction touched the next site?”
  • “Will the pulse rise within three nights?”

6. Scrying

  • Targets cult leaders or dream-marked NPCs.
  • Glimpses include spiral carvings, architectural hints, faction infighting, or flashes of the next site.

7. Divination

  • Ideal for mapping the Web’s progression.
  • Questions like “Which site is the next wound?” guide navigation.

8. Speak with Animals / Plants

  • Animals sense corruption: birds avoid Threads, predators pace nervously.
  • Plants feel underground “pulls” toward active nodes.

9. Legend Lore

  • Reveals a site’s history, completion level, faction origin, and how many nodes remain before catastrophe.

New Ritual Spell: Thread-Step

  • 3rd-level divination (ritual)
  • When cast at a Hollow Mark, reveals the direction, nature, and approximate distance of the next connected node.
  • The caster experiences a brief “spirit wind” tugging them toward the path of the Thread.

How Threads Guide Adventurers

1. Sensory Signs

  • Unnatural cold drafts, faint humming, spiral dust patterns.
  • Animals balk at invisible lines.

2. Environmental Distortions

  • Warped plant-growth direction, odd water ripples, drifting leaves moving “against” the wind.
  • Echoes last longer in these regions.

3. Spirit-Echo Reactions

  • Touching shrines or invoking spirit names may prompt whispers:
  • “Another wound lies east…”
  • “Seven pillars broken… find the sixth…”
  • “The Crown beneath stirs.”

Spirit-Tender Testimony

  • “Things have been feeling strange, colder recently.”
  • “Local spirit has been restless.”
