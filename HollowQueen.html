The Hollow Queen and the Growing Gate

The Queen Beneath

Long before the Age of Echoes, there was a mortal queen — Nerathis of the Hollow Crown — whose hunger for dominion eclipsed all mercy. Legends say she ruled from the mountain fortress of Vey Lethan, where she sought to forge unity through fear. When her seers warned her that the spirits would never kneel, she ordered a massacre upon a mountain town that refused her tithe, slaughtering spirit-tenders, druids, and innocents alike.

That act of cruelty scarred the land itself. The mountain screamed. A seed of corruption took root in the blood-soaked earth, binding her soul to it even as her body perished in the uprising that followed. Her spirit, denied peace, sank into the dark between worlds. There it fed upon pain, ambition, and the echoes of mortal prayer, until it became something other.

Now, deep beneath Veythar, the Queen Beneath dreams and whispers — her will a bridge between this world and a reflection of it beyond, a chaotic realm twisting slowly into a cruel parody of creation.

The Domain Beyond

The realm linked to her essence was once a place of formless chaos — the source of corruption itself. Yet the Queen’s tether has shaped it over the ages, sculpting it into a dark mirror of Veythar’s landscapes: mountains of bone, seas of ink, and forests of whispering glass.

This realm of chaos and corruption, often called the domain beyond or Llygredd, grows more defined each time corruption spills into the mortal realm. Where spirits exist in harmony here, their reflections there are distorted parodies of themselves — driven to devour rather than balance.

The Queen’s Influence

Though bound, the Queen’s will seeps through the cracks between worlds. She cannot manifest directly, but she inhabits mortal minds — artists, scholars, dreamers, and cultists — through dreams and inspiration. Those touched by her influence often become architects of ruin, driven to construct shrines, monuments, or geometric carvings that resonate with the corruption’s frequency.

Each of these sites, when completed, acts as a key-stone in an invisible pattern across Veythar — a vast sigil that will, when complete, open the Growing Gate, merging both realms completely.

Symbols and Signs

Everywhere her touch lingers, one symbol returns again and again — its shape adapted but its pattern consistent:

A spiral descending into a hollow circle, encircled by seven lines that never meet.

Some carve it as a spiral eye, others as a well, or a crown made of broken rings — but always, it echoes descent, unity, and emptiness.

Her followers whisper her names in secret — The Hollow Crown, The Queen Beneath, The Silent Mother, The Voice Below. Common phrases or passcodes might change between groups, but always revolve around the same idea:

  • “The crown turns inward.”
  • “What is buried will reign.”
  • “All echoes end in silence.”

Ritual Sites and Structures

Throughout Veythar, ritual sites are being built, repaired, or unearthed — each drawing from a distinct local culture yet sharing uncanny geometry:

  • Shrines with inverted altars or upside-down sigils.
  • Caves where light never reaches the floor, always spiraling downward.
  • Stone circles with seven broken pillars.
  • Temples where water runs up walls instead of down.

Spirit-tenders call these places the Hollow Marks — points where the veil between worlds thins. Not all are complete, and not all are active… but each pulse makes the Queen’s Gate grow stronger.

Adventurer Clues

Characters investigating corruption might notice:

  • The same spiral symbol etched into unrelated ruins, charms, or cult markings.
  • Recurrent dreams of a woman’s voice speaking from a pool of black ink; smooth except lips that protrude.
  • Shrines whose offerings rot instantly — a sign that the Queen’s influence seeps through.
  • Fractured cult doctrines that all end with the same whispered benediction:
    “May her crown close around the world.”
