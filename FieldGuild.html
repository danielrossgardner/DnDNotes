The Field Guild of Reverberation Studies

Overview:
The Field Guild of Reverberation Studies is a loose, professional association of wizard-scholars who study magical echoes—residual impressions left behind by spirits, shrines, and long-standing natural forces. The Guild is not secret, but little known beyond academic and spiritual circles. Its members live embedded within communities across Veythar, recording observations, comparing patterns, and exchanging papers through correspondence rather than centralized halls. Their work favors careful field study over ritual authority or religious doctrine.

Known Members

  • Mara Dune (Human, Female)
    Location: Red Hollow
    Personality: Brusque, practical, unsentimental
    Study: The cycle of life, decay, and renewal as reflected in lingering spiritual echoes, particularly in harsh or depleted environments.
  • Milo Strand (Halfling, Male)
    Location: Southwest coastal village
    Personality: Calm, patient, quietly contemplative
    Study: Resonance between spirits and celestial bodies—tides, stars, seasonal cycles, and long-duration patterns.
  • Elowen Wardell (Half-Elf, Female)
    Location: Freeport
    Personality: Excitable, highly knowledgeable, enthusiastic organizer
    Study: Compilation, review, and cross-referencing of field notes and papers; maintains Guild correspondence and archives. Owner of a local bookstore.
  • Nessa Quickquill (Gnome, Female)
    Location: Karagost
    Personality: Curious, energetic, analytically minded
    Study: Maritime spirits, currents, tides, and the interaction between sea travel and persistent spiritual phenomena.
  • Gilderoy Leaflock (Gnome, Male)
    Location: Duskwind Forest
    Personality: Easily distracted, enthusiastic, prone to tangents
    Study: Attempts to locate and study the ancient Hemlock spirit at the forest’s heart, though frequently diverted by lesser spirits, ruins, and side phenomena.
  • Toma Reed (Human, Male)
    Location: Willowford
    Personality: Contemplative, organized
    Study: The Old Willow Tree is not just a shrine — it’s a resting place for many lesser spirits, and over centuries, their presence has left resonant imprints in the world. Toma believes these are what he calls “magical echoes.”
