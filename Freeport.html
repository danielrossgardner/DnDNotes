City of Freeport (Shellshore)

Population: ~7,000 (Highly diverse: Humans, Half-Orcs, Drow, Elves, Halflings, and many others)
Primary Trade: International trade hub for goods from the southern archipelago, inland farming produce, precious metals and gems from Vey Irinel, whale oil and bone crafts from Marrowtide, and exotic wares from distant continents.
Tone: A bustling, prosperous port city. Order is tightly enforced by the Merchants’ Guild, yet the city thrives as a crossroads of cultures, songs, and trade. Locals still whisper of its old name, Shellshore, and the turtle spirit that once made its home in Abandon Bay.

Surrounding Geography

  • Abandon Bay: A vast, crescent-shaped bay formed by the turtle spirit’s departure. Freeport sits on its upper right side, overlooking calm waters that open into treacherous sea routes.
  • The Grassy Plain: Rising north of Freeport, a fertile plateau dotted with halfling farmsteads and caravan routes heading inland.
  • Southern Archipelago: Chain of islands to the south. Source of spices, rare woods, and exotic fruits that flow through Freeport’s markets.
  • The Lower Ocean Routes: Distant foreign vessels reach Freeport, making it one of the few cities on the island where truly foreign goods and faces are common.

Layout & Landmarks

  • The Old Quarter (Shellshore) – The original fishing and whaling village. Narrow, twisting streets, haphazardly built stone and timber homes, shrines to the sea, and taverns with weathered whale bones above their doors.
  • The Merchant’s Ring – The organized heart of Freeport. Wide avenues, neat rows of warehouses, guildhalls, markets, and clean plazas. This is where the Merchants’ Guild enforces its authority most strictly.
  • The Shorefront – Bustling docks stretching along Abandon Bay. Ships from the southern archipelago, inland rivers, and far-flung kingdoms all moor here. Guarded day and night by the Shore Watch.
  • The Ascendant Road – A broad, cobbled road climbing gradually from the shore through newer districts up toward the grassy plain. Inns, stables, and caravan yards line the road.
  • The Turtle’s Rest Plaza – A central square honoring the city’s animist roots. At its heart stands a massive stone carving of a turtle’s shell, surrounded by offerings of coins, driftwood, and shells. Locals still refer to the city as Shellshore here.
  • The Guildhall – Headquarters of the Merchants’ Guild. A fortress-like marble and granite hall that also serves as the city’s central bank. From here, strict laws of trade, tariffs, and contracts are administered.

Government & Law

  • The Merchants’ Guild – Holds ultimate authority. Oversees taxation, harbor tariffs, weights and measures, trade licenses, and justice in trade disputes. Wealth equals influence in Freeport.
  • The Shore Watch – A professional city guard funded by the guild. Patrols the docks, markets, and roads to ensure peace and order. Swift and often harsh in dealing with lawbreakers, but widely respected for keeping Freeport safe.
  • Justice of Coin – Laws are based on written guild codes. Punishments lean toward fines, forfeitures, indenture, or exile rather than corporal punishment, keeping labor and trade flowing.

Freeport — Political Landscape

Freeport has long enjoyed the peace that prosperity buys. The Merchants' Guild has kept order through commerce, the Shore Watch has kept order through law, and the occasional pirate threat has been handled by guild marines and a handful of military vessels. That equilibrium is now breaking. A new and organized pirate force is threatening trade routes and beginning to choke the city, and Freeport — never a military power — finds itself underprepared and politically fractured at exactly the wrong moment.

The Factions

The Merchants' Guild — The Good

The Guild is Freeport's governing authority in all but name. It controls trade law, harbor tariffs, the Shore Watch, and the city's small naval force. Until recently this has been sufficient — guild marines aboard merchant vessels and a few military ships have deterred the scattered piracy that occasionally troubled the southern routes. The current threat is something else entirely. The pirates are organized, numerous, and coordinated in a way no one in the Conclave has seen before, and the Guild's military capacity is not built for a sustained naval conflict.

The Guild needs allies and options. It is unaccustomed to needing either.

  • The First Merchant — Supreme authority, public face of the Guild's response to the crisis
  • The Conclave — Three senior members co-signing all major decisions
    • The Ledger — Treasury, contracts, financial pressure on the pirate problem
    • The Warden — Commands the Shore Watch and the Guild's naval forces
    • The Broker — Foreign trade relationships, diplomatic options, outside alliances
  • The General Roll — All licensed guild members in good standing. Can call a Review forcing the Conclave to publicly justify decisions. The threat of it shapes how the Conclave operates.

The Pirates — The Bad

Veythar has known peace with neighboring lands long enough that organized maritime aggression feels almost theoretical to most Freeport citizens. It no longer is. These pirates are not the opportunistic raiders the guild marines have always been enough to handle — they are coordinated, numerous, and operating with a discipline that suggests either military training or a very capable leader, or both. Trade routes to the southern archipelago are already disrupted. If the blockade tightens, the city's flow of goods begins to fail.

Who organized them, where they are based, and what they ultimately want are questions the Guild is only beginning to ask.

The Atheneum of Tides — The Ugly

Established roughly a century ago, the Atheneum of Tides sits at Freeport's southern edge — an odd collection of mismatched buildings enclosed within a low wall, all oriented around a working lighthouse on the southern point. It studies the intersection of arcane theory and the animist spirit energies that define Veythar, and it does so largely in silence. The Atheneum does not seek political influence and has shown little interest in the city's governance over its hundred years of existence. Most Freeport citizens know it mainly as the place with the lighthouse.

The pirate crisis is changing the calculus. The Atheneum controls the southern lighthouse — a navigational asset that becomes strategically significant the moment sea routes are contested. Whether its leadership understands this yet, or what they might want in exchange for cooperation, is unknown. The Guild has not historically needed to ask.

The Conflict

Veythar is not a unified kingdom. Freeport has no standing army worthy of the name and its naval force was sized for peacetime policing, not war. The Merchants' Guild has wealth, law, and influence — none of which stop a well-organized fleet from cutting off your harbor. The Guild must find a way to break or negotiate with the pirates before the blockade becomes a stranglehold, and it must do so without the military infrastructure that a proper nation-state would bring to bear.

The Atheneum of Tides is the tipping point. If the Guild can bring the Atheneum into the conflict on its side, the balance shifts. If the pirates reach the Atheneum first — or if the Atheneum simply refuses to be drawn in — the Guild's options narrow considerably. What the Atheneum wants, and whether it can be persuaded, is the question at the center of Freeport's crisis.

Culture & Society

  • Diversity: Nearly every people of the island and beyond can be found here. Inns and markets bustle with half-orcs from the western coast, drow traders from Vey Irinel, halfling farmers, dwarves from distant mountains, and human sailors from across the sea.
  • Old vs. New: Locals from the Shellshore quarter cling to traditions of whaling songs, tide-offerings, and turtle-spirit lore. New Freeport citizens in the Merchant’s Ring embrace order, prosperity, and commerce above all.
  • Festivals: Trade fairs every spring and autumn coincide with equinoxes, drawing foreign merchants. Summer solstice celebrations still honor the turtle spirit, with boat races across Abandon Bay. In winter, the Guild sponsors a Festival of Lanterns, lighting the harbor in gold.
  • Drinks & Food: Local brews mingle with fine wines from distant shores. Seafood dominates local cuisine—mossbacks, shellfish, and bay crabs—though exotic imports flow in steadily.
