Vae & The Transcendent Court

A closed theocratic empire to the west of Veythar, ruled by an immortal tiefling empress worshipped as the living will of the sun.

The Empress & The Jade Tower

Vae is a theocracy with the empress as the absolute center of worship. The capital rises from a plateau above a verdant valley, its skyline dominated by a jade tower at the plateau's heart — the empress's seat and the empire's sacred axis. She is known only as The Solara; her birth name is forbidden to commoners. She is believed to be immortal — whether through genuine divinity, arcane prolonging, or careful succession theater where each empress simply becomes the next is something outsiders cannot confirm. Her image is everywhere: a tiefling woman wreathed in solar imagery, horns gilded, eyes depicted as literal flames. Worship is mandatory, civic, and woven into every transaction, meal, and sunrise ritual.

The Caste Tiers

Society is rigidly stratified by blood and proximity to the tower. Tieflings occupy the upper castes, further divided by the color of their heritage — those with gold or red tones ranking above those with cooler hues. Half-breeds occupy uncomfortable middle tiers, useful but never fully trusted. At the bottom are the Tabaxi, who perform all labor the upper castes consider beneath dignity. They are not formally enslaved — the law is careful about this — but their movement, marriage, and profession are all state-regulated. Rebellion is rare and punished as heresy rather than crime, which carries far worse consequences.

The Closed Shore

Vae does not trade openly. Foreign ships are permitted only at a single designated port on the eastern coast called Sun Haven, where all goods are inspected and all travelers confined to a walled district. No one ventures inland without an imperial escort that doubles as a warden. Exports are carefully controlled in quantity — enough to create dependency without revealing anything about the interior. What little is known of Vae has been pieced together from trade intermediaries and the rare Tabaxi who have escaped.

  • Alchemical compounds of unclear origin that apothecaries find useful but cannot reverse-engineer
  • Perfumed oils derived from rare plateau flowers unavailable elsewhere
  • Dyed silks in deep reds and blacks that hold color unlike anything Veythar weavers produce
  • Volcanic glass vessels and mirrors, prized for their clarity and dark sheen
  • Preserved spiced meats and exotic dried fruits from interior agriculture

The Sun Doctrine

The theological architecture of Vae is total. The empress is not merely favored by a god — she is the sun's will made flesh, and the sun is not a spirit in the animist sense but a sovereign force of absolute order. Darkness, disorder, and silence are heresy. The Tabaxi's closeness to nature and animist tendencies are framed in imperial doctrine as proof of their spiritual inferiority — they hear echoes because they cannot bear the full light.

Scholars in Veythar who have studied fragments of Vae texts note an unsettling tension: the doctrine is obsessively focused on suppressing something, though what specifically is never named directly. Only that the flame must never go out.
