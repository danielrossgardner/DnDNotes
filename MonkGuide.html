Combat & Monk Quick Reference

Compact guide: what you get each turn, available action types, monk specifics, and a short example opener.

What you get on your turn (combat)

Action Bonus Action Movement Reaction

There is also free object interaction — e.g., opening a door while moving is a free object interaction.
Note: opening a door, closing it, and locking it behind you would require an Action.

Actions (examples)

Common Actions

  • Attack
  • Cast a Spell
  • Dash — double your movement.
  • Disengage
  • Dodge — focus on not getting hit (attacks against you have disadvantage).
  • Help — assist someone else with a task.
  • Hide
  • Search
  • Use an object — whenever using an object requires an action (example: lighting a torch).

Picking up a torch is typically not an action; lighting it is.

Bonus Actions

  • Certain spells or magical items specify they use a bonus action.
  • Unarmed Strike — after making an attack, immediately make one unarmed strike.
  • Patient Defense — spend 1 Ki to take the Dodge action as a bonus action.
  • Flurry of Blows — spend 1 Ki to make 2 unarmed strikes (must be immediately after an attack).
  • Step of the Wind — spend 1 Ki to take Disengage or Dash as a bonus action.

Reactions

  • Opportunity Attack — someone leaves or passes within 5 ft. of you; you can make one attack.
  • Deflect Missiles — deflect or catch a non-magical missile; reduce damage by 1d10 + Dex + monk level.
    If damage is reduced to 0 you catch the missile and may spend 1 Ki to throw it back.

Callouts & Teamwork

On your turn in combat, call out short instructions to your party: cover me, follow my lead, focus the big one, etc. Short tactical calls help coordinate support and prevent you from being isolated.

Monk Details

  • Ki points = your monk level.
  • Recovery: 30min rest you get Ki back.

Example opener (in combat)

Use your extra movement to close the distance on the enemy. Strike them with a javelin, then use a Ki point for Patient Defense. This means you have the enemy locked down — attacks against you have disadvantage. If the enemy tries to flee you would get another strike (opportunity attack). Hopefully your team then comes in and supports you.
